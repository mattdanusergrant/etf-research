Group 1 · Robot-body

🦿 Human-shaped robot makers

The most focused — and the riskiest — bet on humanoid robots.

BOTTThemes Humanoid Robotics

Oldest and cheapest pure humanoid fund — mostly Korea/China names, plus Tesla.

+37% this yr · 0.35% fee (cheapest) · $32M · since 2024 · 41 holdings · automatic

👍Cheapest pure-humanoid bet, with the longest track record of this group.
⚠️Heavy in Chinese & Korean names — exposed to tariffs and trade disputes.
HUMNRoundhill Humanoid Robotics

The only fund where experts hand-pick the holdings — a confident, Tesla-led bet on robot makers.

Too new for a yearly return · 0.75% fee (high) · $73M · since 2025 · 41 holdings (68% ex-US) · experts pick

👍Active managers adjust the picks instead of tracking a fixed list.
⚠️Priciest of the focused funds, and heavy in Chinese & Korean names.
KOIDKraneShares Humanoid & Physical AI

Biggest body fund — and a twist: it buys robot parts makers (motors, sensors, chips), not the robots.

Too new for a yearly return · 0.69% fee · $224M (largest focused) · since 2025 · 60 holdings · evenly weighted

👍"Sell shovels" — bets on parts every robot needs, spread evenly so no name can sink it.
⚠️Misses out if one robot brand wins big. Holds no Tesla.
Group 2 · All-robot

🦾 Every kind of robot

Factory, surgical and warehouse robots plus some humanoids — bigger, steadier, easier to trade.

BOTZGlobal X Robotics & AI

The largest and most liquid robot fund — big, established names, led by NVIDIA.

−2% this yr · 0.68% fee · $3.7B (huge) · since 2016 · 52 holdings · automatic

ROBOROBO Global Robotics & Automation

The original robot fund and the most diversified — your money spread across the most companies.

+18% this yr · 0.95% fee (highest) · $1.5B · since 2013 · 80 holdings (most) · automatic

👍Most diversified and lowest-drama way to own robots — 80 names, none too large.
⚠️Highest fee in the guide, and being so spread out caps upside in a boom.
